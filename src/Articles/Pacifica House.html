The Pacifica House was a secret society at Brown University. As early as 2005, Pacifica House has claimed a link to the 19th century Franklin Society. Evidence supports the existence of Pacifica House from 2003 to 2010.

Alleged History

Lineage

Pacifica House's history, according to itself, proposes links between a handful of well documented, but chronologically disparate, historical societies at Brown. The earliest archived copy of PacificaHouse.org cites the Franklin Society (1824-1834) as the genesis of Pacifica House, followed by the Cammarian Club (1893-1971), which morphed into the Undergraduate Council of Students.1

New Curriculum

Pacifica House claimed on its website to be responsible for the “New Curriculum” of 1969.1 This claim is likely the source of speculation that the Curriculum's chief architect, Ira Magaziner, was a member of Pacifica House.2

Activities

2002 Grade Inflation Report

In December 2002, Pacifica House published Grade Inflation and Brown University: An Urgent Report.3 The report, in part, provided the impetus for a College Curriculum Council investigation on the adoption of a plus-minus grading system.4 The grade inflation report is the earliest artifact of The Pacifica House's existence.

2003 Spring Weekend Incident

During 2003 Spring Weekend, a Pacifica House member allegedly shadowed a UCS member for the durration of the weekend in an attempt at intimidation.5

2003 UCS Election Rules Protest

In protest over a change of election rules, Justin Sanders ’04, a representitive on the Undergraduate Council of Students resigned from his post during the last meeting the council.5

2010 Criticisms of Banner

A Pacifica House publication criticized the effects of Banner on course registration.6, 7

2010 Criticisms of Fall Weekend

Pacifica House was credited with a Fall 2010 insertion of flyers into student mailboxes regarding the naming of Fall Weekend.7.

Anecdotal Evidence of Continued Recruitment

Computer science undergrad Eli Rosenthal claimed to know somone who was asked to join Pacifica House in 2014.

PacificaHouse.org

PacificaHouse.org was registered on September 9, 2004.

By Feb 3, 2005 (the earliest date for which an archive of the page exists), PacificaHouse.org featured a mature website detailing a purported group history, structure, and membership rules.1In the following three years, the site underwent only minor, editorial revisions in its text. During this period, Pacifica House identified itself with variations of the seal of Pembroke College of Oxford University:

The August 26, 2006 capture brought a slight change of the crest; its bottom-right quadrant was recolored from blue to red.

Around May 2009, PacificaHouse.org's webmaster temporarily replaced the site's previous landing page with one designed for another service, "Timeless Canvas". Inquiries were directed to jess@timelesscanvas.com.3

By June 16, 2009, the webmaster corrected that error, replacing the erroneous landing page with the single-image landing page that remains to this day.4.

Press Coverage and Online Discussion

Alex Carnevale '05 on Secret Societies

On September 14, 2004, the Brown Daily Herald's Opinion Editor Alex Carnevale criticized the apparent elitism of the Pacifica House, comparing it to Yale's Skull and Bones society. 5 The editorial cites an incident at the Spring 2004 Spring Weekend involving Pacifica House, and names Justin Sanders as an estranged member. The Inning Club has not yet reached Carnevale or Sanders for a statement. Carnevale's editorial was the subject of criticism.

CollegeConfidential Forum Topic

On October 20, 2004, Brunonian9 wrote on a collegeconfidential.com forum6,

there is a new controversy at brown, dunno if you all have heard about it... it's about "pacifica house" which is the secret society and a lot of people hate it for just being all around jackasses on campus. if you go to browndailyherald.com and do a search on "pacifica" you'll read a lot of columns against it. it's own website is

www.pacificahouse.org

what do you think about secret societies on college campuses, esp. the ivy league schools?

Ben Clark '05 on Pacifica House

On October 21, 2004, a letter to the editor by Ben Clark '05 entitled "Baseball, you are killing me" was published in the Brown Daily Herald, referencing "the mysterious force that is Pacifica House". 7 This is the earliest mention of Pacifica House on the Brown Daily Herald. The Inning Club has not yet reached Ben Clark for a statement.

BDH: Brown A to Z

On September 1, 2005, a tongue-in-cheek handbook to "brownspeak" by the Brown Daily herald defined Pacifica House as "A society so secret you can visit their website."8

Matt Gelfand on Criticisms of Banner

In a February 7, 2010 letter-to-the-editor, Matt Gelfand '08 applauded Pacifica House's criticism of the effect of Banner on course registration.6,10The Inning Club has not yet reached Matt Gelfand for a statement or found a copy of the Pacifica House's statements that he references.

Ra Ra Brunonia

In 2011, Blog Daily Herald writer Jenny Bloom published a brief synopsis on Pacifica House's activities, citing the criticism of Banner and Fall Weekend as two recent examples.7.

Known Members

Jesse Maddox

In Fall 2014, an error in re-registering PacificaHouse.org revealed Jesse Maddox as the site's webmaster.11 Maddox attended Brown from 2004 to 2008, during which he co-founded the Janus Forum along with professor John Tomasi. The 2003 publication of the grade inflation report rules Maddox out as a founding member.
