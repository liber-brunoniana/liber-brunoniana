The Literary Magazine of Brown University appeared in two issues only, in May 1967 and March 1968. The contents of the magazine were mostly poetry interspersed with art work and photography. George Hodgkins ’68 and Peter Sevenair ’68 were the editors, and the names of Steven Heller ’69 and Christopher Coles ’69, founders of the Hellcoal Press, appear as literary editor and member of the literary board. The magazine was superseded by the Hellcoal Press Pamphlet Series.
