Pi Kappa, was a junior society, said to be “ldquo;reinstituted in 1900.”rdquo; In the Liber Brunensis, beginning in 1901, the list of members of Pi Kappa appears, under this heading, “ldquo;1864-1894 Re-Instituted 1900.”rdquo; Whether Pi Kappa existed at an earlier date is uncertain. The Brown Paper includes in November 1864 and again in November 1865 a society called “ldquo;Pi Kappa Fraternitas,”rdquo; with a list of members whose names are decidedly fictitious. The revived society, if such it was, presented during Junior Week in April 1901 a farce entitled “ldquo;Through Thick and Thin”rdquo; in Lyman Gymnasium, which the Brown Daily Herald described as “ldquo;not at all pretentious in plot or length,”rdquo; but “ldquo;bright and witty.”rdquo; The annual farce became a tradition of Pi Kappa, which also assumed the function of host to visiting athletic teams. New members were traditionally announced at the junior prom in a ceremony at midnight in which they were “ldquo;tapped”rdquo; by the outgoing members. When Pi Kappa was replaced by the Brown Key in 1930, the retiring members tapped the new Key members.
